Information about Diseases

Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic condition that affects how your body turns food into energy. It can be genetic or result from lifestyle factors. Prevention involves maintaining a healthy diet, regular exercise, and monitoring blood sugar levels.

Heart Disease

Heart disease, often caused by atherosclerosis, can result from factors like high blood pressure and high cholesterol. Prevention includes a heart-healthy diet, regular exercise, and avoiding smoking.

Kidney Stones

Kidney stones are hard deposits that form in the kidneys. They may develop from concentrated urine and various factors. Staying hydrated and maintaining a balanced diet can help prevent kidney stones.

Liver Disease

Liver diseases can be caused by viruses, alcohol misuse, or obesity. Prevention involves moderating alcohol intake, maintaining a healthy weight, and getting vaccinated for viral hepatitis.

Parkinson's Disease

Parkinson's is a neurodegenerative disorder. While its exact cause is unknown, both genetic and environmental factors play a role. There is no cure, but treatment aims to manage symptoms and improve quality of life.

Lung Cancer

Lung cancer often develops due to smoking, exposure to secondhand smoke, or environmental factors like asbestos. Quitting smoking and avoiding exposure to harmful substances can reduce the risk.

Hepatitis C

Hepatitis C is a viral infection that spreads through contaminated blood. Prevention involves avoiding sharing needles, practicing safe sex, and getting vaccinated for hepatitis A and B.

Stroke

Strokes can result from blocked or burst blood vessels in the brain. Risk factors include high blood pressure and smoking. Prevention includes maintaining a healthy lifestyle, managing blood pressure, and not smoking.
